## Maker comment (first comment)

Hey Product Hunt 👋

Last winter a friend's basement flooded. I wanted to help, and I knew a dozen other people did too — but the second you start a GoFundMe, the surprise is dead. The recipient sees the page, sees the awkward "donor" list, and the whole thing turns into a charity moment instead of what it actually is: their people showing up.

So we built **Drop**.

You start a private drop for someone who's going through it. You invite the crew by email. Everyone pledges an amount in secret. Then on drop day, at the time you picked, everyone gets a one-tap email that opens Venmo with the right handle, the right amount, and a note pre-filled. They tap send.

The recipient wakes up to their phone lighting up with Venmo after Venmo from everyone who loves them. They never see Drop. They never knew it was coordinated. It just feels like the world rallied for them at once.

A few details we sweated:
- The recipient is **never** a user. No account, no notification, nothing.
- Contributor links are unguessable — no one can stumble in.
- Live progress images render in the emails themselves, so the crew sees the energy build without opening the app.
- It feels like a group chat planning a surprise party, not a fundraising platform.

Free while we're in launch. Would love your feedback — especially if you've ever wanted to do something like this and didn't know how.

## FAQ
**Does the recipient know?** No. That's the whole point.
**Why Venmo?** It's where the money already lives. We don't hold funds, we don't take a cut.
**Do you charge?** Not right now.
**What if someone doesn't send on drop day?** Organizer can nudge. Pledges are honor-system — same as a group chat.
